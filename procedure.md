<b>Requirements (Instruments, Chemicals & Other) : </b> <br>

1.	Conical flask <br>
2.	HCL <br>
3.	NaOH <br>
4.	Sucrose <br>
5.	Phenolphthalein indicator <br>
6.	Fehling A solution <br>
7.	Fehling B solution <br>
8.	Analytical Balance <br>
9.	Sulphuric acid <br>
10.	Starch powder <br>
11.	1% α-amylase enzyme solution <br>
12.	Measuring cylinder <br>
13.	Distilled water <br>
14.	Volumetric flask <br><br>

<b>Procedure : </b><br>

<b>Standardization of Fehling solution : </b> <br>

1.	Weigh 4.75 gm sucrose and transfer to a conical flask. <br>
2.	Pour 50 ml distilled water and add 5 ml conc. HCl, and allow to stand for 24 hr. <br>
3.	Neutralise the solution with 1N NaOH using phenolphthalein. <br>
4.	Transfer the solution to volumetric flask and makeup the volume to 500ml with water. <br>
5.	Take 25 ml solution into the 100 ml volumetric flask and make up the volume with water. <br>
6.	Take 5 ml of each Fehling A and Fehling B solutions in a conical flask, and add 10 ml water in the Fehling solution mixture. <br> 
7.	Titrate the Fehling solution mixture with the standard invert sugar solution under boiling condition, using methylene blue indicator. <br>

<b>Analysis of sample : </b> <br>

1.	Prepare 0.5% starch solution (Weigh 2.5 gm starch powder, transfer it to a 500 ml volumetric flask, dissolve with water and make the volume to 500ml) <br>
2.	Take 7 conical flask and pour 50 ml starch solution in each flask <br>
3.	Add 5 ml α-amylase enzyme solution (1%) in each flask <br>
4.	Treat the solution with enzyme for 0, 5, 10, 15 & 20 min and mark the flask T0, T1, T2, T3 and T4 respectively <br>
5.	Titrate the Fehling solution mixture with each of the enzyme treated starch solution  <br>
